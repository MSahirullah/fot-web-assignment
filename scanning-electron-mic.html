Scanning Electron Microscope

A scanning electron microscope (SEM), a type of microscope, is designed for directly studying the surfaces of solid materials with high resolution. The SEM projects and scans a focused high-energy beam of electrons over a surface. The signals that drive from electron-sample interactions reveal information about the sample including external morphology (texture), and chemical composition of the sample.
